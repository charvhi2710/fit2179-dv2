Violent Crime in Malaysia

Where the cases sit, where the risk sits, and what those places have in common.

01 — Setting the scene

What we mean by “violent crime”

Violent crime is not one thing. It includes the everyday property losses that empty out a wallet, and the rarer, deeper violations that reach into a person directly. The Royal Malaysia Police records both, across every district in the country.

02 — Volume vs risk

Two ways to count the same country

The most populated places carry the loudest count. But scale is not the same thing as risk — and adjusting for population reveals a different geography of harm.

03 — The flip, made visible

When the question changes, the ranking flips

If volume tells one story and risk tells another, the cleanest way to see it is side by side. On the left, every state is ranked by how many cases were recorded. On the right, by how often a case touches one in every hundred thousand residents. Two states do something dramatic.

04 — What kind of crime is where

Each state has its own crime signature

Not every state experiences the same shape of harm. Some places see crime that wears down homes and vehicles; others see crime that reaches further, into the body itself. Each state's profile tells you something about its economy, its density, and its day-to-day rhythm.
